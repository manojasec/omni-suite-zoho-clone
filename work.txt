Honest answer first: Zoho ships 55+ products across sales, finance, HR, IT, productivity, BI, and developer platforms. What we have is roughly 8 of the most common SaaS modules — comparable to Zoho One's core "CRM + Books + Desk + Projects + Campaigns" bundle, but nowhere near full parity. Trying to "build all of Zoho" in one go is unrealistic; the right move is to identify gaps, then pick the next 1–3 modules to add depth.

Recommended order to tackle next
Realistic, high-value, fits the stack:

Inventory & purchase orders — extends our Books module; ~3–5 days of focused work.
Expense management — small-scope, finishes the finance triangle (Invoice + Inventory + Expense).
HR / Employees / Leaves — a popular Zoho One pillar; clean Prisma extension.
E-signature workflow — high perceived value, mostly UI + status state machine; integrate with our existing PDF generation.
Bookings — calendar slots + public booking page; reuses public-forms pattern.
Bug tracker — small fork of Helpdesk + Projects.
Live chat widget — embeddable script + ticket-creation pipeline; ties to Helpdesk.
Marketing automation (journeys) — extend Campaigns with a workflow runner.
Mail (IMAP-backed inbox) — significant scope, requires background workers.
Reports v2 — pivot/custom dashboards on top of existing analytics.
